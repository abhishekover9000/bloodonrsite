General Questions

What is BlooDonR?

We are a new health tech company, specifically providing a platform to evolve the blood bank market. With our platform we interconnect hospitals, laboratories and donors into one overlapping healthcare system.

What happens to my blood, in most cases, after I donate to a nonprofit, hospital and/ or laboratory?

In most scenarios, the blood you donate is being sold on what BlooDonR has entitled, “The Blood Bank Market”. Most blood pints donated are sold anywhere from $200.00 to as high as $1,500.00 per pint.

How can a nonprofit profit from selling blood?

Non-profits are allowed to provide retail, services and create revenue streams, as long as their financial balance is zero by the end of each tax year, they are within the terms of the law.

Does BlooDonR transport, store, test, transfuse, handle or draw blood?

The answer is NO. There are many restrictions, limitations and laws that attempt to secure the interaction with blood from donors, hospitals and laboratories. Since BlooDonR is not ready to involve ourselves in that capacity, BlooDonR as of now will remain a technology platform company.

Donors Questions

How old do I have to be to donate blood?

In the United States of America, you must be 17 years of age in order to donate blood, or 16 years old with parents’ permission.

What are you doing with my personal information?

BlooDonR does not sell or distribute personal information such as contact information, to any third party. We are a transparent company that would like to bring that mentality to the health care industry. Collaboration is our game and we think that by having everyone on the same page when it comes to donating blood, is just the beginning of journey.

How much money do I get for my donation?

Each donation through our platform is a minimum of $50.00 to $100.00 per donation, from either hospitals or laboratories. If you do not use our platform and donate at your own free will, there is not guarantee of compensation with a minimum basis.

How do I receive my financial compensation gift?

Once you register, locate a donation alert, donate your blood, the facility must test and verify the quality of your blood. If your blood is qualified as acceptable in accordance with the law, you will then receive your compensation on our platform. If your blood is denied due to your blood not being transferable, then you will receive nothing. However, if your blood can be used for testing research then you will receive a minimum of $25.00 per your donation.

Hospitals Questions

How does BlooDonR benefit my facility?

Hospitals are spending tens of millions of dollars a year on purchasing blood, with BlooDonR we provide an outlet for hospitals to save money a minimum 50% in total cost for purchasing blood, due to our full transparency policies between hospitals, laboratories and donors. For more information, please click here , in order to contact us and to speak with one of our representatives.

What if my facility does not have a blood center or a fully approved FDA blood center?

Simple. We provide you with a direct connection and communication with laboratories that have agreed to our set price points in testing blood, storing blood and blood transportation. With BlooDonR, hospitals will always have the best price points from laboratories, while also building solid foundations for collaboration, data sharing and much more.

Who has access to the program within our facility?

Usually the CEO/ Director of the hospital facility/ laboratory and the CEO/ Director of the blood center, will have primary access to your account. Any other personnel to be added to our profile must be requested to your primary point of contact within BlooDonR.

Labs Questions

What is the “set price point” policy?

Every laboratory has its own way of charging people and hospitals for their testing, storage and transportation. When you register with BlooDonR, each laboratory will abide by our policy that will give neighboring hospitals the BEST price for your services. With this policy, laboratories will see an increase in the quantity of services hospitals would request from them, providing a steady stream of business between labs and hospitals.
